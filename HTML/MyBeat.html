R&B

R&B stands for Rhythm and Blues, which is a genre of popular music that originated in African American communities in the United States in the late 1940s. It combines elements of jazz, gospel, and blues music, often characterized by soulful vocals, strong rhythms, and emotional lyrics. R&B has evolved over the years and has influenced many other genres, including hip hop, pop, and contemporary R&B.

Pop Rock

Pop rock is a music genre that blends pop music's catchy melodies with rock's energetic instrumentation. It features upbeat rhythms, memorable hooks, and relatable lyrics covering various themes like love, relationships, and personal experiences. Popularized in the 1970s, pop rock continues to be a favorite due to its broad appeal and infectious tunes.

OPM

OPM stands for "Original Pinoy Music," which refers to Filipino music that is composed, produced, and performed by Filipino artists. It encompasses various music genres, including pop, rock, hip-hop, R&B, ballads, and folk music, among others. OPM celebrates Filipino culture, language, and identity through music, and it plays a significant role in the local music industry, reflecting the diversity and creativity of Filipino musicians and songwriters.
